CTThinker2.0 is a tool to teach students important CT skills.
There are in total 5 modules with 15 levels each.
Module 1 focuses on abstraction: identifying relevant information in a problem.
Module 2 focuses on decomposition: dividing a problem into multiple subproblems that are easier to solve.
Module 3 focuses on pattern regonization: finding structures or similarities between problems.
Module 4 focuses on algorithmic thinking: constructing a systematic way of solving a problem.
Module 5 combines the 4 previous aspects together, fostering CT skills.

There are 3 types of levels.
In type A levels you will have select all answers from a given set of boxes.
To mark a box, click it with your mouse once, click it again to unmark it.

In type B levels you will have to enter the answer yourself inside a box.
To enter the answer, first click outside the window, then click inside the window, and lastly enter the answer with keyboard.

In type C levels you will have to drag the boxes into a correct order.
To drag a box, put the cursor on the box and hold the mouse key, then move it around.
